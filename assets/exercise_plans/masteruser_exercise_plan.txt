## Test User's 7-Day Exercise Plan (July 27, 2024 - August 02, 2024)

**This plan considers your preference for home/outdoor workouts, menstrual cycle considerations, and aims to meet your fitness goals.**

**Notes:**

* **Warm-up:** 5-10 minutes of light cardio, such as brisk walking or jumping jacks, followed by dynamic stretching like arm circles and leg swings.
* **Cool-down:** 5-10 minutes of static stretching, holding each stretch for 30 seconds.
* **Intensity:** Moderate intensity is defined as feeling slightly out of breath and able to carry on a conversation.
* **Hydration:** Drink plenty of water throughout the day, especially during exercise.
* **Listen to Your Body:** Rest when you need to and don't push yourself too hard, especially during your menstrual cycle.

**Day 1: Saturday - Focus on Strength & Flexibility**

- **Morning:** 30 minutes of outdoor walk at a brisk pace.
- **Evening:** 30 minutes of strength training, focusing on lower body:
    - Squats: 3 sets of 10-12 repetitions.
    - Lunges: 3 sets of 10-12 repetitions per leg.
    - Glute bridges: 3 sets of 15-20 repetitions.
    - Plank: 3 sets of 30-60 seconds.
- **Yoga:** 20 minutes of gentle yoga flow focusing on flexibility and relaxation.

**Day 2: Sunday - Rest and Recovery**

- **Rest Day:** Focus on active recovery like stretching, walking, or gentle yoga. 
- **Optional:** 30 minutes of light swimming or cycling if preferred.

**Day 3: Monday - Cardio and Core**

- **Morning:** 30 minutes of cardio, choosing from:
    - Jumping rope: 3 sets of 30 seconds, followed by 30 seconds of rest.
    - High knees: 3 sets of 30 seconds, followed by 30 seconds of rest.
    - Burpees: 3 sets of 10-12 repetitions.
- **Evening:** 20 minutes of core workout:
    - Crunches: 3 sets of 15-20 repetitions.
    - Bicycle crunches: 3 sets of 15-20 repetitions.
    - Russian twists: 3 sets of 15-20 repetitions.
    - Plank: 3 sets of 30-60 seconds.

**Day 4: Tuesday - Strength & Flexibility**

- **Morning:** 30 minutes of outdoor walk at a brisk pace.
- **Evening:** 30 minutes of strength training, focusing on upper body:
    - Push-ups: 3 sets of as many repetitions as possible.
    - Pull-ups (or assisted pull-ups): 3 sets of 8-10 repetitions.
    - Rows (using resistance bands or dumbbells): 3 sets of 10-12 repetitions.
    - Tricep dips (using a chair or bench): 3 sets of 10-12 repetitions.
- **Yoga:** 20 minutes of gentle yoga flow focusing on flexibility and relaxation.

**Day 5: Wednesday - Cardio & Core**

- **Morning:** 30 minutes of cardio, choosing from:
    - Running or jogging: 30 minutes at a moderate pace.
    - Cycling: 30 minutes at a moderate pace.
    - Stair climbing: 3 sets of 10-12 minutes.
- **Evening:** 20 minutes of core workout:
    - Plank variations: 3 sets of 30-60 seconds each.
    - Side plank: 3 sets of 30-60 seconds per side.
    - Leg raises: 3 sets of 15-20 repetitions.

**Day 6: Thursday - Strength & Flexibility**

- **Morning:** 30 minutes of outdoor walk at a brisk pace.
- **Evening:** 30 minutes of strength training, focusing on full body:
    - Squats: 3 sets of 10-12 repetitions.
    - Lunges: 3 sets of 10-12 repetitions per leg.
    - Push-ups: 3 sets of as many repetitions as possible.
    - Rows (using resistance bands or dumbbells): 3 sets of 10-12 repetitions.
    - Plank: 3 sets of 30-60 seconds.
- **Yoga:** 20 minutes of gentle yoga flow focusing on flexibility and relaxation.

**Day 7: Friday - Rest and Recovery**

- **Rest Day:** Focus on active recovery like stretching, walking, or gentle yoga. 
- **Optional:** 30 minutes of light swimming or cycling if preferred.

**Menstrual Cycle Considerations:**

* **During your period:** Focus on low-impact exercises like walking, swimming, or yoga. Avoid high-intensity workouts and listen to your body.
* **Premenstrual phase:** You may experience fatigue and bloating. Reduce intensity or shorten workout duration as needed. Focus on gentle stretching and restorative yoga.
* **Post-menstrual phase:** You may have increased energy levels. You can gradually increase the intensity and duration of your workouts.

**Important Points to Keep in Mind:**

* This is a sample plan, feel free to adjust based on your personal preferences and needs.
* Consult with a doctor or healthcare professional before starting any new exercise program, especially if you have any underlying health conditions.
* Listen to your body and don't hesitate to rest when needed. 
* Gradually increase the intensity and duration of your workouts as you get fitter.
* Be consistent with your exercise routine to see results and achieve your fitness goals. 
* Have fun and enjoy your workouts!
